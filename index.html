Milestones in Space Exploration

1

Launch of Sputnik 1

Event: First artificial satellite launched by the Soviet Union.

Date: October 4, 1957

Details: Sputnik 1’s launch marked the beginning of the Space Age, sparking the space race.

2

First Human in Space – Yuri Gagarin

Event: First human to travel into space and orbit Earth.

Date: April 12, 1961

Details: Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin's Vostok 1 mission was a pioneering achievement in human space exploration.

3

Launch of Apollo 11

Event: First human landing on the Moon.

Date: July 20, 1969

Details: Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon, making history.

4

Launch of the Hubble Space Telescope

Event: Hubble begins sending back detailed images of distant space.

Date: April 24, 1990

Details: Hubble transformed our understanding of the universe, unveiling distant galaxies and cosmic phenomena.

5

Landing of Mars Rover Curiosity

Event: Curiosity rover lands on Mars to explore the planet.

Date: August 6, 2012

Details: NASA’s Curiosity rover explored Mars to gather data on its geology, climate, and potential for life.

6

Voyager 1 Enters Interstellar Space

Event: First spacecraft to enter interstellar space.

Date: September 12, 2013

Details: Voyager 1 left the Sun's influence, venturing into the vastness of interstellar space.

7

First Image of a Black Hole

Event: Event Horizon Telescope captures the first image of a black hole.

Date: April 10, 2019

Details: The first-ever image of a black hole, located at the center of the Messier 87 galaxy, was revealed.

8

China’s Chang’e 4 Lands on the Moon's Far Side

Event: First successful landing on the Moon’s far side.

Date: January 3, 2019

Details: China’s Chang’e 4 mission achieved the first-ever landing on the Moon's far side, an extraordinary feat in lunar exploration.

9

SpaceX Launches First Crewed Mission

Event: First privately operated spacecraft with astronauts onboard.

Date: May 30, 2020

Details: SpaceX's Crew Dragon launched NASA astronauts to the International Space Station, a landmark event for private space companies.

10

NASA’s Perseverance Rover Lands on Mars

Event: Perseverance rover lands to search for signs of past life.

Date: February 18, 2021

Details: Perseverance’s mission is to collect samples and search for signs of ancient microbial life on Mars.

11

NASA’s Artemis I Moon Mission

Event: Uncrewed test flight of NASA’s new lunar spacecraft.

Date: November 16, 2022

Details: Artemis I tested the Space Launch System and Orion spacecraft, paving the way for future crewed missions to the Moon.

12

China’s Chang’e 5 Brings Moon Samples to Earth

Event: China successfully returns lunar samples to Earth.

Date: December 17, 2020

Details: Chang’e 5 delivered the first new lunar samples to Earth since the 1970s, a monumental achievement for China’s space program.

13

James Webb Space Telescope Launched

Event: The most powerful space telescope ever launched.

Date: December 25, 2021

Details: The JWST is set to revolutionize our understanding of the universe, including the formation of stars, galaxies, and potentially habitable planets.

14

NASA’s Dragonfly Mission to Titan

Event: Dragonfly mission will launch to Titan, Saturn’s moon.

Date: Target launch date: 2027

Details: The Dragonfly rotorcraft will explore Titan, one of the most intriguing moons in the solar system, to study its prebiotic chemistry and possible habitability.
